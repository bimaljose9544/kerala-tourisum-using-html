MAIN PLACES

Kerala Tourism – God's Own Country Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is one of the country's most popular tourist destinations, known for its breathtaking natural beauty, unique culture, and rich traditions. Often referred to as “God’s Own Country”, Kerala offers a diverse range of experiences—from tranquil backwaters and serene beaches to lush hill stations and vibrant festivals. The state's backwaters, especially in Alleppey and Kumarakom, offer visitors a chance to explore scenic waterways in traditional houseboats. The cool hill stations of Munnar and Wayanad are perfect for those seeking peace and greenery, with tea plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries. Kerala’s coastline stretches for over 550 kilometers, with pristine beaches like Varkala and Kovalam attracting tourists from around the world. Kerala is also famous for its rich cultural heritage, including classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and the ancient martial art of Kalaripayattu. Traditional Ayurvedic treatments and wellness resorts are another major attraction, offering holistic healing experiences. The state’s cuisine, with its emphasis on coconut, spices, and seafood, is a treat for the senses. Popular dishes like appam with stew, Kerala sadya, and Malabar biryani showcase the diversity of local flavors. Kerala’s commitment to sustainable tourism and eco-friendly practices has earned it global recognition. Whether you are seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural exploration, Kerala promises a memorable and enriching experience.

EVENTS

Onam
Kerala’s biggest festival, celebrated around August–September.
Marks the harvest season and the return of King Mahabali.
Features Vallamkali (boat race), Pookalam (flower decorations), Onasadya (feast), and cultural programs.
Thrissur Pooram
Held in Thrissur, it is one of the most spectacular temple festivals.
Famous for caparisoned elephants, melam (drums and music), and fireworks.
Vallam Kali (Snake Boat Races)
Traditional boat races held during the monsoon, especially in Alappuzha and Aranmula.
Part of Onam celebrations, attracting large crowds and tourists.
Vishu
Malayalam New Year, celebrated in April.
Features Vishukkani (first sight), fireworks, and traditional feasts.
Sabarimala Pilgrimage
Held annually during November to January in Pathanamthitta.
Devotees trek to the Sabarimala temple of Lord Ayyappa.
Kerala Boat Festival Season
Runs from July to September.
Major races: Nehru Trophy (Alappuzha), Aranmula Uthrattathi Vallamkali, etc.
Kerala International Film Festival (IFFK)
Held in Thiruvananthapuram every December.
Showcases world cinema and attracts global filmmakers.
Attukal Pongala
Celebrated in Thiruvananthapuram, it’s a women-only festival.
Women gather to cook a special offering (pongala) in public spaces.
Theyyam Festival
Popular in Kannur and Kasaragod.
A ritual dance form where performers become “possessed” by deities.
Christmas & Easter
Celebrated widely by the Christian community with local customs.
